About Me

I’m a first-generation American, born and raised in Brooklyn, New York, but my roots run deep to the Essequibo Coast of Guyana, where my parents immigrated from in 2003, the same year I was born. Being the first in my family born in the U.S., I proudly wear my Guyanese heritage on my sleeve—it’s a vital part of who I am.

Caribbean music has always been the heartbeat of my home and my connection to culture. My father’s collection of CDs filled our small apartment with the vibrant sounds of old-school reggae, soca, and calypso. Some of my fondest memories are of singing and dancing with my sister as we mimicked the rhythmic moves of the Caribbean, creating our own choreographies to the tunes. Those melodies weren’t just music; they were a bridge to my heritage, a reminder of where I come from, and a celebration of who I’m becoming.

This website is my way of honoring that legacy and sharing the rich tapestry of Caribbean music and culture with the world. Whether you're a lifelong fan or discovering these genres for the first time, I invite you to explore, learn, and feel the rhythms that have shaped me.
